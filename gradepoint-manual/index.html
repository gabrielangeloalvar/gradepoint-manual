Overview

What is GradePoint?

GradePoint is a simple web app that helps students track their grades, compute their GPA, and monitor academic progress across subjects — all in one clean dashboard.

This manual follows Norman's Model of Interaction and the ACM HCI Framework to explain both how to use the app and why it works the way it does.

Flow A — Adding a Grade (Step by Step)

1
User Taps "+ Add Grade"

The button is always visible on the dashboard. Clear affordance — it looks clickable and is labeled plainly.

2
User Fills in Subject & Score

A simple form opens with just two fields. Score field only accepts numbers 0–100 to prevent errors before they happen.

3
System Validates & Saves

System checks the score is within range, assigns a letter grade (A/B/C/D/F), saves to the database, and recalculates GPA.

4
Output Grade Card Appears + GPA Updates

New grade card shows instantly with a color badge. GPA counter animates to the new value. A green toast says "Grade saved ✓" for 3 seconds.

Flow B — Deleting a Grade

1
User Swipes left on a grade card (mobile) or clicks ✕

Delete is accessible but not accidental — requires a deliberate swipe or a specific click.

2
System Removes grade, recalculates GPA

Grade is removed from database. GPA is instantly recalculated without the deleted entry.

3
Output Card fades out + Undo toast for 5 seconds

User can tap "Undo" within 5 seconds to restore the grade — supporting Norman's principle of error recovery.

ACM Principle — Error Recovery: GradePoint never permanently deletes data immediately. The 5-second undo window ensures accidental deletions can always be reversed, reducing user anxiety.
